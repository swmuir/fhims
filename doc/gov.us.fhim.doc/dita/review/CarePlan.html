A column is provided at the far right of the table for you to enter comments related to each data element. If the class or domain is missing data elements, please add a row at the bottom of the sheet and specify a name, description, and type of data (e.g. numeric, string, date/time, code).

Once you have reviewed the data elements, please review the value sets associated with the coded data elements. Please provide feedback by adding comments in the column provided at the right of item/row or by adding rows at the bottom table. For any new coded concepts without a valid code yet, our team will propose them for addition in the source coding system.

AcceptanceReview.patientPreference

A statement expressed by the patient or a proxy for the patient describing how he or she would prefered to be treated. Note that this concept is modeled as a class in the HL7 DAM, which includes attributes such as the strength of the preference, reason, date range, and an indication of why the preference might not have been fulfilled. The FHIM models these concepts collectively as a single text property because it was deemed to be unlikely that any current or near-future systems would need to compute on such properties of the patient's preferences.

"A care preference is a statement expressed by the patient, custodian or caretaker responsible for the patient in order to influence how their care is delivered. A preference expresses a personal choice and may be driven by cultural, religious and moral principles. As such it is a principal component of patient centered care and autonomy. Care preferences serve as modifiers of the Care Plan which influence how the plan is personalized for the individual. A care preference may be specified prospectively to influence future care planning and treatment or it may be expressed and recorded at arbitrary decision points during interventions. A preference expresses a request to fulfill a patient's choice or desire. The choice may be a strong and absolute statement such as an end of life directive. The request could also be a desire to be fulfilled if possible given care team capabilities and resources." - HL7 Care Plan Domain Analysis Model

ActivityOutcomeReview Class

This class documents an evaluation of whether the activities that were undertaken have helped the patient meet their health goal. In other words, "did what we did make a difference?"

"An activity outcome review measures the result of individual implemented action (observational or interventional) against goal success criteria. The action outcome review might address only a subset of goal success criteria." - HL7 Care Plan Domain Analysis Model

WARNING: it is held by some that the efficacy of the Activity towards achieving the Goal is not necessarily knowable, rather one can only know whether the Goal was achieved or not. Therefore, it has been recommended that this class be deleted.

CarePlanEpisode Class

This class represents a set of planned actions and goals for a Patient dealing with a given set of issues or concerns. The set of problems being managed may be complex and intertwined, and the Care Plan Episode will likely cover an extended period of time. However, the Care Plan Episode differs from the Longitudinal Care Plan in that the Longitudinal Care Plan covers all Care Plans over the life of the Patient, including those that have been terminated because the undlying condition had been resolved. The Care Plan Episode is focused on the set of issues that is being managed over a particular period of time. Note that the HL7 Care Plan DAM includes a confidentiality property that we do not model, as confidentiality is expected to be handled by constructs in the Security and Privacy domain.

"The Plan captures the shared attributes for Care Plan, Plan of Care and Treatment Plan." - HL7 Care Plan Domain Analysis Model

"Describes the intention of how one or more practitioners intend to deliver care for a particular patient for a period of time, possibly limited to care for a specific condition or set of conditions." - HL7 FHIR, CarePlan resource.

Communication Class

This class documents an interaction between two or more Care Team Members regarding the Care Plan for the patient. This is where the negotiation occurs between providers, or where the care team decides on a course of action during a periodic review.

"Communication is ongoing during care coordination. Note that the DAM has a "pertainsTo" property which "Links the communication to some clinical object which is the target of the communication". This property is of type clinical object, which the FHIM doesn't have at the moment. It could probably be linked to Observation in the future.

GoalSuccessCriterion Class

A desired future state, expressed as an Observation. Note that this class is a subtype of Observation, although this observation is for a future state. In other words, this is an Observation in a "Goal" mood (in HL7 V3 speak). The observation date would in this case be the target date of the success criterion, not the date that an actual observation was made. Note also that the value is an Any so it can handle textual goals (like “I want to walk my daughter down the aisle for her wedding”) as well as coded or structured goals such as “HbA1c below 7”. The latter kind of goal could potentially be tested against lab results in an automated fashion.

"Defines criteria which must be met to determine goal achievement." - HL7 Care Plan Domain Analysis Model

HealthGoal Class

An objective intended to be realized by the successful execution of the Care Plan.

"Describes the intended objective(s) of carrying out the Care Plan." - HL7 FHIR, Care Plan Resource.

"A health goal specifies a future target or achievement towards which the effort of care planning and execution is directed. Goals represent concrete targets to reduce or eliminate concerns or risks. A Goal may exist in the absence of concerns or risks. For example, a patient may have a goal to improve their fitness level. The plan always has at least one goal." - HL7 Care Plan Domain Analysis Model.

Note that a goal might be constructed of sub-goals, which might each be associated with a single outcome. For example, a goal to loose 5 pounds per week over 6 weeks might spawn 6 child goals to loose 5 pounds in week 1, week 2, etc. Each child goal would be associated with one actual outcome (i.e., lost 4 pounds in week 1; lost 6 pounds in week 2, etc). Then the "parent" goal outcome could be the total (e.g., lost 28 pounds in 6 weeks).

PlanReview.reasonForReview

This optional property describes the circumstances that triggered the review. The assumption is that this property would generally only be used to describe non-routine reasons why a review is/was required. Regularly scheduled reviews would not need to be indicated in this text. For example, a review might be indicated due to a change in the condition of the patient that causes a requirement to revise the care plan.

PlanSteward.entity

Identifies the Person or Organization who is Participating as a Plan Steward. This property is a pointer to an Entity rather than a Provider because the Plan Steward could conceivably be the Patient him/herself or a member of the Patient’s family, whereas elsewhere in the FHIM, most Participation classes assume the Participating Entity is a Provider.
